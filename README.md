My work sits at the intersection of classical geotechnical engineering and data-driven methods. My thesis focuses on scientific machine learning (SciML) and creating digital twins for soil behaviour—training predictive models on large-scale, generated geotechnical datasets to reduce reliance on expensive laboratory testing and empirical correlations.

Alongside my research, I build automation tools for PLAXIS 2D. I script full FEM workflows from Excel input to result extraction, parametric studies, and Factor of Safety calculations—taking work that normally requires hours of manual interaction and reducing it to a single, efficient pipeline. I am also currently involved in a registered startup and in the process of filing a related patent.
